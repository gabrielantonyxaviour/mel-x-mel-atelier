# Execution Packet

## Project

Title: **Mel x Mel Atelier: Three Disagreeing Mels Build One Campaign**

Short description: Three versions of Melius agent Mel act as competing creative directors for one impossible campaign brief. A fourth editor Mel critiques them, rejects one, merges the winner, and preserves Gabriel's human taste edit in the final campaign kit.

Thesis: **Melius is not just a generator. It is the room where creative judgment becomes visible.**

Primary subject: Post-FTX Bahamas tourism reboot.

Fallback subject: Crystal Pepsi comeback campaign.

## README Source

This project demonstrates a Melius canvas workflow where:

1. A messy campaign brief enters the canvas.
2. Commercial Mel optimizes for clarity and conversion.
3. Art Mel optimizes for visual originality and memorable restraint.
4. Contra Creator Mel optimizes for portfolio shareability and public launch.
5. Editor Mel critiques all three branches, rejects the weakest, merges the winner, and writes a visible decision note.
6. Gabriel adds a human taste edit so the final direction is not outsourced to the agent.

The repo contains the public case-study wrapper, submission copy, scripts, and build proof. The canonical creative artifact remains the Melius canvas.

## Persona Prompt Seeds

Commercial Mel:

> You are the conversion-focused creative director. Your job is to restore trust without repeating the scandal. Prioritize booking intent, clarity, and credible proof. Avoid pity, apology language, crypto references, and luxury cliches.

Art Mel:

> You are the visual-originality director. Your job is to make the campaign feel sun-bleached, quiet, and impossible to confuse with a resort stock ad. Prioritize texture, restraint, negative space, and one unforgettable frame.

Contra Creator Mel:

> You are the shareability and portfolio director. Your job is to make the campaign easy to explain in one Contra post. Prioritize a memorable before/after story, a headline that screenshots well, and a case-study arc.

Editor Mel:

> You are the critic and merger. Score each branch for trust, originality, shareability, and dignity. Reject one branch with a specific reason. Merge the strongest two into a final campaign direction and preserve the human taste edit.

## Melius Feedback Draft

Melius is strongest when the canvas is treated as a creative room instead of a prompt box. The node layout made it natural to keep parallel directions visible at the same time, which is exactly what this project needed: the disagreement between branches mattered as much as the final campaign.

The agent workflow was useful because Mel could take different jobs in the same canvas: brief interpreter, creative director, critic, and editor. The most interesting moment was not a generation result; it was the critic step where the editor branch rejected a direction for making the scandal the hero.

The friction point is legibility. A canvas that is useful for working can become hard to read as a submission artifact, especially when multiple branches, media nodes, and comments are all visible. For challenges like this, a presentation/export mode that preserves comments and node relationships at thumbnail scale would make judging much easier.

The positive surprise is that Melius rewards showing process instead of hiding it. The rejected branch, the comment, and the human taste edit all became part of the story rather than internal production notes.
